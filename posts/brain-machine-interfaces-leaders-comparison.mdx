The world changed forever when the internet was invented, allowing near instantaneous access to a wealth of information previously only available by hunting through books. It changed again when this information became accessible through a device small enough to fit in your pocket. Now humanity begins the next step in our evolution.

Brain-machine interfaces (BMIs) have the potential to revolutionise the way we interact with technology and the world around us. Each aspect of our lives - the way we work, communicate, live day to day - will be drastically altered as BMIs allow us to communicate directly with computers and other devices through our thoughts and movements.

Not only could this allow us to communicate instantaneously, interact with our devices or access any information on the internet via a thought, but it could also change the medical world. There have already been developments in the research of using a BMI to control prosthetic limbs, and that’s just the beginning.

One company many of us know already, at the forefront of this technology, is Neuralink - founded by the more recently controversial Elon Musk and a team of neuroscientists and engineers. Neuralink is currently developing two pieces of equipment: one is a chip that would be implanted into the skull - with electrodes linking to the brain, the other is a robot that could automatically implant the chip.

However, Neuralink is not alone in their pursuit to bring BMIs to the rest of the world. [Kernel](https://www.kernel.com) and [Paradromics](https://www.paradromics.com) are two other companies that are also developing similar technologies, centred around communication between the brain and external devices.

Paradromics is focused on developing high-bandwidth BMIs that can transmit large amounts of data. The company, similar to Neuralink, is working on implantable devices. However, they are also developing non-invasive technologies. Both options aim to be usable for a wide range of applications, from virtual reality experiences to medical treatments.

Contrastingly, Kernel is focused primarily on non-invasive BMIs that use electroencephalography (EEG) to measure brain activity. They’re working on a range of applications for this technology, such as creating new ways for people to interact with external devices, as well as improving cognitive function and memory.

All three companies are working towards similar goals, yet take differing approaches to the development of their BMIs. Kernel focuses solely on non-invasive devices, while Paradromics is working on both implantable and non-invasive, and Neuralink focuses on only implantable devices.

In terms of current research and development, Neuralink has made significant strides in recent years. The company has successfully implanted devices in animals and has conducted initial human trials, and is working towards creating a fully implantable BMI system that could be used to treat neurological conditions such as Parkinson's disease. Paradromics is also making progress in its research, and is currently conducting preclinical trials of its technology. Kernel, meanwhile, is in the early stages of development, but has raised significant funding and has a team of experienced scientists and engineers working on its technology.

While the potential benefits of BMIs are significant, there are also potential risks and ethical considerations that must be taken into account. These include the risks of hacking or malfunction, as well as the potential for misuse or abuse of the technology. It is important that these issues are carefully considered and addressed as these companies continue to develop their technologies.

In conclusion, Neuralink, Paradromics, and Kernel are all working towards the goal of creating technology that allows for communication between the brain and external devices. While they are taking different approaches to achieving this goal, they are all making progress in their research and development. It will be interesting to see how these companies continue to shape the future of BMIs and the role they play in shaping the way we live and interact with the world.
